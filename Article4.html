Physics: Simple Calculations

Worked examples of simple Physics calculations with step-by-step explanations.

1. Motion

1. Calculating Speed

Speed = Distance / Time

Example: A car travels 100 m in 5 s.

  • Distance = 100 m
  • Time = 5 s
  • Speed = 100 ÷ 5 = 20 m/s

2. Calculating Acceleration

a = (v − u) / t

Example: Velocity increases from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 s.

  • u = 10 m/s, v = 30 m/s
  • t = 5 s
  • a = (30 − 10) ÷ 5 = 4 m/s²

2. Forces

3. Force Using Newton’s Second Law

F = ma

Example: A 2 kg object accelerates at 3 m/s².

  • m = 2 kg
  • a = 3 m/s²
  • F = 2 × 3 = 6 N

3. Energy & Work

4. Work Done

W = Fs

Example: A force of 10 N moves an object 5 m.

  • F = 10 N
  • s = 5 m
  • W = 10 × 5 = 50 J

5. Kinetic Energy

KE = ½mv²

Example: A 2 kg object moves at 4 m/s.

  • m = 2 kg
  • v = 4 m/s
  • KE = ½ × 2 × 4² = 16 J

4. Pressure & Density

6. Density Calculation

ρ = m / V

Example: Mass = 8 kg, Volume = 2 m³.

  • ρ = 8 ÷ 2 = 4 kg/m³

7. Pressure Calculation

P = F / A

Example: Force = 100 N, Area = 5 m².

  • P = 100 ÷ 5 = 20 Pa

5. Electricity

8. Electric Current

I = Q / t

Example: Charge of 10 C flows in 5 s.

  • I = 10 ÷ 5 = 2 A

9. Voltage Calculation

V = W / Q

Example: 20 J of energy transfers 4 C of charge.

  • V = 20 ÷ 4 = 5 V

10. Electrical Power

P = VI

Example: Voltage = 10 V, Current = 2 A.

  • P = 10 × 2 = 20 W
